SAMSON was supposed to come online Jan 1 1973.

This did not happen.

  • budget ballooned; costs overrun
  • Software (message switching) finished in 1976
  • 1977-78 contract disputes halt work completely
  • Had to hire an American firm for the terminals, eventually
  • by 1985 all mentions of SAMSON disappear from the records; it was tested, it was online, and then... we hear of it no more.

So in a limited way, it was a success for the half dozen years it ran. But in a larger sense, it was a failure because it never became anything greater. Why did it not generate new possibilities?

OK But the Story of the Canadian Internet is Not Over Yet!

or,

tcp/ip enters the chat

Canadian Universities go with tcp/ip

  • terminals, mainframes;
  • bulletin board services;
  • independent regional networks of universities emerge
    • these get wired together as NetNorth
  • NetNorth gets a dedicated circuit into BITNET 1985
  • 1989 first search engine 'Archie' developed at McGill ('Archi -v +e')
  • Fibre optics: Quebec universities get high speed connections in 1989; by 1991 everyone else does too as part of 'CANARIE', some 23000 km of fibre optic cable

Thus, the Internet in Canada:

  • absolutely could not have arrived without the microwave relay network in the 1950s: the idea of interconnecting systems was obvious, in a Canadian context
  • could've arrived in the 1960s/70 as the military SAMSON network
  • did arrive in the 1970s with dataroute/datapac and the X.25 protocol
  • in popular memory, arrives with universities and BITnet
  • in daily use, was already lurking in videotext tv terminals
  • Officially arrives with NetNorth for commercial everyday consumers
  • National internet as internet tcp/ip arrives in 1990s with fibre optic cables and 'Canarie'
  • University high performance computing networks still exist, managed by the Data Alliance of Canada, where your personal computer becomes a terminal to a supercomputer.
